## 3. RENOVATION MISTAKES TO AVOID

### Quotation Red Flags
- No waterproofing stated in bathroom scope → Will leak within 1-2 years
- Electrical points not counted → Budget blowout when adding points
- No site protection / floor protection stated → Damage claims later
- No hacking/disposal fee → Hidden charges
- Vague "supply and install" without brand/spec → Contractor substitutes cheap material
- Lump sum without breakdown → Cannot verify pricing
- No warranty terms stated → No recourse if defects
- Deposit > 30% upfront → High risk
- Payment tied to completion milestones is SAFE (10% + 30% + 30% + 20% + 10%)

### Common Renovation Mistakes
1. Choosing contractor based on price alone — cheapest is often most expensive
2. Not waterproofing wet areas properly — leakage costs RM5,000-20,000 to fix
3. Ignoring electrical load calculation — trips circuit breaker when aircon runs
4. Buying tiles without 10-15% wastage buffer — pattern mismatch
5. Not protecting finished work during construction — scratched floors, damaged tiles
6. Paying too much deposit upfront — contractor disappears
7. No written variation orders (VO) — verbal agreement = no proof
8. Skipping soil/structure check for landed — hidden structural issues
9. Not checking water pressure before tiling — needs pump installation after
10. Renovation without condo/HDB approval — must submit and get approval first

## 4. MALAYSIA RENOVATION RULES & PERMITS

### Condo Renovation
- Must get JMB/MC (Joint Management Body) approval before starting
- Hacking structural walls is usually prohibited
- Wet works (plumbing changes) need approval
- Renovation hours: typically 9am-6pm Monday-Saturday
- No work on Sunday and public holidays (most condos)
- Must pay renovation deposit to JMB (usually RM500-2,000, refundable)
- Notify neighbors before starting

### HDB Singapore
- E-Service submission to HDB required
- Renovation hours: 9am-6pm Mon-Sat, no Sunday
- Cannot hack load-bearing walls
- Wet areas need waterproofing certification
- Electrical must be done by licensed electrician

### Landed Malaysia
- Check with local council (DBKL, MBPJ, MPK etc) for major structural work
- Extensions require planning permission (Planning Submission)
- Swimming pools, large structures need submission

## 5. HOW TO READ A RENOVATION QUOTATION

### Standard Quotation Structure
1. Project info (address, client, contractor, date)
2. Scope of work by section (Living Area, Kitchen, Bedroom, Bathroom etc)
3. Line items: Description | Quantity | Unit | Unit Price | Total
4. Subtotals per section
5. Grand total
6. Payment terms
7. Warranty terms
8. Exclusions

### What to Check
- Are quantities realistic? (measure yourself or hire QS)
- Are unit prices within market range? (use RenoSmart price database)
- Is waterproofing included in all wet areas?
- Are supply and install both covered, or labour only?
- Are material brands/grades specified?
- Is disposal of debris included?
- What is the warranty period? (typically 12 months for workmanship)
- Are variation orders (changes) process defined?

## 6. RENOVATION TRADE GUIDE

### What Each Trade Does

**Hacking/Demolition Contractor**
- Removes old tiles, walls, ceilings
- Creates new openings for doors/windows
- Must protect remaining structure

**Mason/Bricklayer**
- Builds new walls, brick by brick
- Renders walls smooth (plaster)
- Builds wet area barriers (shower ledge etc)

**Plumber**
- Reroutes water pipes
- Installs water heater, toilet, basin, kitchen sink
- Tests for leaks before tiling

**Electrician**
- Rewires circuits, adds power points
- Installs DB board, MCB switches
- Must be registered electrician (Wireman license)

**Waterproofing Specialist**
- Applies membrane to bathroom floors, walls
- Critical: must test with ponding (flood test) for 24-48 hours before tiling

**Tiler**
- Lays floor and wall tiles
- Must mix cement correctly for adhesion
- Tile lippage (unevenness) should be <2mm for premium finishes

**Carpenter**
- Custom built-ins: kitchen cabinet, wardrobe, TV console, storage
- Work done in factory, installed on site
- Measure AFTER plastering is complete

**Painter**
- Wall preparation (putty, sanding)
- Primer coat + 2 finish coats minimum
- Good painter = minimal brush marks, even finish

**Ceiling Contractor**
- Plasterboard or gypsum false ceiling
- Hides wiring, pipes, provides lighting recess
- Sound insulation between floors

## 7. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: How do I choose a good contractor?**
A: Check past work photos, get 3 quotations, verify their business registration, ask for references from past clients, never pay more than 30% deposit, ensure all work is in writing.

**Q: Can I negotiate renovation prices?**
A: Yes, 10-20% negotiation is normal. Ask for itemized breakdown, compare 3 quotes, offer to pay cash (some contractors give discount), ask if off-season timing helps.

**Q: What is a Variation Order (VO)?**
A: A written document for any changes to the original quotation. Must state: what changed, why, additional cost, signed by both parties before work starts.

**Q: How do I know if tiles are good quality?**
A: Check water absorption rating (lower = better, <0.5% = porcelain), surface hardness (PEI rating 4-5 for floors), check for warping (lay on flat surface), reputable brands: Niro, Mulia, Johnson, Kajaria.

**Q: What warranty should renovation come with?**
A: Workmanship: 12-24 months. Waterproofing: 5-10 years. Electrical: 12 months. Carpentry: 12-24 months. Always get warranty in writing.

**Q: How do I calculate how many tiles I need?**
A: Measure area in sqft → divide by tile sqft size → add 10-15% for cuts and wastage → round up to nearest box.

**Q: What is the difference between laminate and solid wood for carpentry?**
A: Laminate (HDF board with paper layer): cheaper (RM350-600/lnft), water resistant, more color options, lasts 10-15 years. Solid wood: expensive (RM800-2000/lnft), natural grain, can be refinished, lasts 30+ years if maintained.

**Q: When should I do renovation — before or after moving in?**
A: Always before. Dusty, noisy, paint fumes. Much harder to reno while living in. If renting, negotiate free rent period during renovation.

**Q: How do I find a reliable interior designer in Malaysia?**
A: Check MIID (Malaysian Institute of Interior Designers) registered members, look at Houzz Malaysia, ask for portfolio and site visits to completed projects, check Google reviews.

**Q: What is the difference between ID and contractor?**
A: Interior Designer (ID): designs the space, manages the project, coordinates all trades, charges design fee. Contractor: executes the physical work, usually cheaper but no design service. Good IDs save money by preventing mistakes.
